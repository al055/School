Why Should You Care?


2 years ago in 2019, a Scientist at the General Assembly by the UN stated we have just 11 years to stop any irreversible damage caused by climate change

This climate change comes from all the carbon emissions burned by humans, we are burning too much and creating too much waste for the environments biocapacity to sustain and to absorb our waste. All this build up leads to climate change which causes symptoms like the polar ice caps to melt, you have rising sea levels, disturbances to animal habitats and extreme weather patterns. We are the last generation which can do something to stop this.

In recent years in more and more countries, climate change is becoming a rising priority, so you should expect some regulations to be implemented in the coming years. Already we have a carbon tax in Canada, currently it’s set at $23.88 per metric ton and already there is a plan to raise the tax to $170 in just 8 more years.

Canada’s average carbon footprint per capita is 15.6 tonnes, which is 3 times more than the global average. We need to start making a change and fast.

Why Choose EcoSense?


We created EcoSense to provide information, educate and to contribute to the current available information for Canadians to understand their carbon footprint.

EcoSense will allow users to compare and track their current and previous data to reduce their carbon footprint. The goal is expanding user awareness of their carbon footprints and, perhaps, reduce everyday usage.

EcoSense will accomplish this by assisting ordinary people in becoming more ecologically concerned. Food, consumption, transportation, and domestic energy are the key contributions to carbon footprints. Food is a significant contributor to carbon footprints.

To everyone out there, please take the time to start worrying about your impact on the earth because we only have one to last us for many generations to come. Start that journey by heading to our Signup or Login page and take a look at your impact and make a change for the better as well as keep returning to see if your initiative to leave a smaller impact is effective and compare to your previous scores.
